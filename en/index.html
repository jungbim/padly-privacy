1. Introduction

Padly is an iOS application that helps travelers in Korea understand and find Korean menstrual products. The Service provides product information, product-matching support, nearby store guidance, and educational guide content. Padly does not provide medical diagnosis, medical treatment, medical advice, fertility advice, menstrual-cycle prediction, or any claim that a product prevents, treats, or cures a medical condition.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how long we keep it, when it may be shared with service providers, and what rights users may exercise. The Policy is designed to support Apple App Store submission requirements and to address applicable privacy laws, including Korea’s Personal Information Protection Act (“PIPA”) and, where applicable, the EU General Data Protection Regulation (“GDPR”).

Apple states that App Store privacy responses must describe the data collected by the app and by third-party partners, and that developers are responsible for keeping those responses accurate and up to date.1

2. Information We Collect

Padly follows a data-minimization approach. In the current v1 implementation, the main personal data categories are account authentication data, foreground location used for nearby store search, and technical/service logs generated by infrastructure providers. Padly does not collect menstrual-cycle dates, symptoms, age, photos, camera data, payment card data, advertising identifiers, or background location in v1.

4. Legal Bases for Processing Under GDPR

Where GDPR applies, we rely on the following legal bases under Article 6 of the GDPR.2

Processing activity GDPR legal basis
Account creation, sign-in, and session management Performance of a contract or steps prior to entering into a contract, Article 6(1)(b)
Optional foreground location for nearby store search Consent, Article 6(1)(a), expressed through the iOS location permission flow
Security, debugging, and service reliability Legitimate interests, Article 6(1)(f), provided those interests are not overridden by the user’s rights and freedoms
Responding to legal requests or statutory obligations Legal obligation, Article 6(1)(c)
Support inquiries voluntarily sent by users Consent or legitimate interests, depending on the nature of the inquiry

Padly does not intentionally process special-category health data under GDPR Article 9 in v1. Menstrual product matching in Padly v1 is treated as product information and retail guidance, not as medical diagnosis or health profiling.

7. International Data Transfers

Padly may use service providers located outside the user’s country, including the United States. Where GDPR applies and personal data is transferred internationally, we will rely on appropriate safeguards such as adequacy decisions, Standard Contractual Clauses, or other lawful transfer mechanisms as applicable.3

Users should understand that privacy and data-protection laws in other countries may differ from those in their country of residence. We take reasonable steps to use reputable infrastructure providers and to limit transferred data to what is necessary for the Service.

9. Account Deletion

Users may request account deletion by using the in-app account management flow, if available, or by contacting us at the email address below. After verifying the request, we will delete or de-identify account data unless retention is required for legal, security, dispute-resolution, or legitimate operational reasons.

Logging out only removes the active session from the device; it does not delete the account. Deleting the app may remove local session data from the device, but it does not automatically delete the account stored with the authentication provider.

10. Children’s Privacy

Padly is not directed to children. The Service is not intended for children under 14 in Korea or under the applicable minimum digital-consent age in the user’s jurisdiction. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child without appropriate consent, we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

11. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the nature of the data, including HTTPS transmission, managed authentication infrastructure, and limited data collection. However, no system can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Users should use a strong password and should not reuse passwords across services.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy to reflect changes in the Service, legal requirements, Apple App Store requirements, or our data practices. We will provide notice of material changes through the app, website, App Store listing, or other reasonable means. If a change materially affects user rights or introduces new categories of personal data processing, we will provide additional notice or request consent where required.
